Cloud Fraction and Deforestation in the Amazon

Introduction

Deforestation in the Amazon is currently one of the greatest environmental threats. Land use, typically in agriculture and energy production have led to deforestation and have presented severe loss in biodiversity as well as increases in CO2. Losses in Earthâ€™s biosphere due to deforestation have been clear and prevalent, but the effects to the atmosphere may be overlooked. Changes in land can cause severe shifts in climate and weather patterns. In its most extreme case, deforestation could lead to desertification. By analyzing satellite images across the Amazon Basin, we can quantify how the atmosphere may be affected by deforestation.
